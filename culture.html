A Melting Pot of Flavors

African Influence

From the rich stews to the use of ground provisions (root vegetables) like yams and cassava, the African influence is foundational. Dishes like Callaloo and Coo-Coo (similar to polenta) are direct descendants of West African cuisine.

Indian Influence

Indentured laborers from India brought with them spices, curries, and rotis. Today, curry is a staple, and "Roti shops" are ubiquitous. Doubles, the most popular street food, is a chickpea curry served on fried flatbread.

Chinese & Other Influences

Chinese immigrants introduced stir-frying and soy sauce, leading to unique "Trini-Chinese" dishes. Syrian and Lebanese communities contributed gyros and spices. The result is a truly global cuisine that is distinctly Trinidadian.
